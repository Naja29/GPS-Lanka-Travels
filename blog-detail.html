Top 10 Things to Do in Sri Lanka
for First-Time Visitors

GPS Lanka Travels March 2025 10 min read 2,840 views

Sri Lanka is one of the most rewarding destinations in Asia — a compact, teardrop-shaped island that packs an extraordinary range of experiences into every kilometre. Ancient ruins sit beside misty tea plantations, tropical beaches stretch below jungle-covered mountains, and wildlife roams through national parks just hours from bustling colonial cities.

Whether you have 10 days or 3 weeks, here are the top 10 things you absolutely must do on your first visit to the Pearl of the Indian Ocean.

Sri Lanka's breathtaking hill country — tea estates, mist and the iconic Nine Arch Bridge at Ella

1. Climb Sigiriya Rock Fortress

Sigiriya — the "Lion Rock" — is Sri Lanka's most iconic landmark. A 5th-century rock fortress that rises 200 metres above the surrounding jungle, it was once a royal palace, complete with gardens, frescoes, and a mirror wall. The climb takes around 90 minutes and rewards you with panoramic views that stretch to the horizon on all sides.

Visit early morning (6:30–7:00 AM) before the heat and crowds arrive. The golden light at sunrise on the surrounding plains is simply unforgettable. Combine it with nearby Dambulla Cave Temple for a full day in the Cultural Triangle.

2. Ride the Scenic Kandy to Ella Train

Often called the most beautiful train journey in the world, the 7-hour ride from Kandy through the highlands to Ella passes through emerald tea estates, misty mountains, and past waterfalls that tumble beside the tracks. Hang out of the open door as the train winds through cloud forest — it's one of those rare travel experiences that lives up to the hype.

Book 2nd class observation car tickets in advance through the Sri Lanka Railways website. The journey is best done during the day — the afternoon light through the tea hills around Nuwara Eliya is magical.

3. Safari in Yala National Park

Yala has one of the highest concentrations of leopards in the world. A dawn or dusk jeep safari gives you an excellent chance of spotting these elusive big cats, along with elephants, sloth bears, water buffaloes, crocodiles, and hundreds of bird species. Book with a reputable operator and go in the dry season (February–July) for best wildlife visibility.

4. Watch Blue Whales at Mirissa

Between November and April, the waters off Mirissa are one of the best places in the world to see blue whales — the largest animals on Earth. Spinner dolphins, sperm whales, and fin whales also frequent these waters. Choose a responsible operator that follows whale watching guidelines and keeps a respectful distance.

5. Hike Ella Rock & Little Adam's Peak

Ella is the hill country gem that every traveller falls in love with. Ella Rock (a 3-hour return hike through tea estates and jungle) rewards you with sweeping views of the Ella Gap and surrounding mountains. Little Adam's Peak is shorter and more accessible — perfect for sunrise. And the Nine Arch Bridge, a colonial-era stone viaduct surrounded by tea bushes, is one of the most photographed spots in Sri Lanka.

6–10: More Must-Do Experiences

6. Explore the Galle Fort — A UNESCO World Heritage Site and the best-preserved colonial fort in South Asia. Walk the ramparts at sunset, explore the boutique-lined cobblestone streets, and sip a cold drink at one of the cafés overlooking the Indian Ocean.

7. Visit the Temple of the Tooth Relic in Kandy — Sri Lanka's most sacred Buddhist site, housing a tooth relic of the Buddha. The evening Puja ceremony (at 6:30 PM) is a deeply moving cultural experience with drumming, chanting and offerings.

8. Surf Arugam Bay — On the east coast, Arugam Bay is one of Asia's top surf destinations. Even if you don't surf, the laid-back beach village vibe, fresh seafood, and stunning lagoon sunsets make it worth the journey.

9. Explore Jaffna & the North — Sri Lanka's most underrated destination. Ancient Hindu temples, colourful tuk-tuks, extraordinary food, and pristine island getaways. Jaffna is a completely different side of Sri Lanka that few tourists reach.

10. Take a Cooking Class — Sri Lankan cuisine — rice and curry, hoppers, kottu roti, pol sambol — is extraordinary. A hands-on cooking class with a local family is one of the most memorable experiences you can have on the island.
